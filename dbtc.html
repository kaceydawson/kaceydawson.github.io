Dental Buyer Transition Consulting

Supporting You With Your Practice Purchase

Purchasing a practice is a multi-step complex process that requires thorough planning, analysis, and careful execution. Our Practice Consultants provide a structured methodology crafted from decades of experience to assist clients throughout this process. This Consulting service is limited term in scope to assist practitioners “from the beginning to the end” of the purchase process and is a good fit for practitioners who need support during practice evaluation and acquisition but do not need ongoing consulting services.

Leverage our knowledge to help you achieve your goals!

Our Process

Client Assessment: Understand client objectives, financial capacity, and preferred practice attributes.

Assemble Expert Team: Assist Client in identifying and selecting external experts to support the purchase process.

Networking & Research: Utilize industry contacts, dental brokers, and online listings to locate several suitable practices.

Preliminary Evaluation: Assess 3-5 potential practices based on key factors such as location, patient demographics, financial performance, and growth potential.

Financial Analysis: Review financial statements, tax returns, and production reports to evaluate profitability and financial concerns.

Operational Assessment: Review practice operations, including staff structure, patient flow, and scheduling efficiency.

Valuation Methods: Utilize industry-standard approaches (Price-toCollections, Price-to-Earnings) to determine reasonable market value for the Client.

Asset Evaluation: Assess tangible (equipment, real estate) and intangible (goodwill, patient base) assets for valuation analysis. Discuss with the client any possible updates they feel are needed.

Lender Identification: Connect clients with specialty lenders experienced in dental practice acquisitions.

Loan Application Support: Assist in preparing the necessary documentation for loan applications. (Note: Loan advisory services are provided free of charge in CA, NV, VT, and ND; in other states, specialty banks may pay referral fees to ThePracticeConsultant.com.)

Negotiation Strategy: Develop a negotiation approach aligned with client objectives.

Coordinate Drafting the LOI: Work with the client to draft and finalize the Letter of Intent with the seller.

Legal Documentation: Collaborate with legal professionals who will draft and review the Asset Purchase Agreement. Client is responsible for all legal costs.

Lease or Real Estate Contracts: Assist in negotiating lease terms or real estate purchase agreements which are then drafted and reviewed by Client’s Attorney as applicable.

Assist with a Successful Closing: Support the final closing process.

Operational Handover: Help Client develop a brief transition plan/timeline for operations post-purchase.

Additional Information

Consulting Fees: $9,500 total, with $5,000 due upfront and the remainder upon successful closing.

Outside Additional Costs: The client is responsible for all external professional services (legal, accounting, financial advisory, brokerage as applicable etc.), as well as all transition-related expenses (moving, marketing, rebranding, training, and system upgrades).

Regular Communication: Ongoing communication via text and email.

Consultations: Periodic consultations throughout the process (up to 14 hours total, including phone, email, and Zoom).

Performance Monitoring: Ongoing Tracking of key performance indicators to ensure financial and operational targets are met.

Continuous Improvement: Provide ongoing coaching and strategic advisory support services on growth and operational enhancements and major Practice decisions.
